Divergence and Curl Properties

$\vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) = \vec{b} \cdot (\vec{c} \times \vec{a}) = \vec{c} \cdot (\vec{a} \times \vec{b})$
$\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) = \vec{b}(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c}) - \vec{c}(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})$
 $\nabla \times (f\vec{v}) = f(\nabla \times \vec{v}) + (\nabla f) \times \vec{v}$
$\nabla (\vec{v} \cdot \vec{w}) = \vec{v} \times (\nabla \times \vec{w}) + \vec{w} \times (\nabla \times \vec{v}) + (\nabla \cdot \vec{v})\vec{w} + (\nabla \cdot \vec{w})\vec{v}$
$\nabla \cdot (\vec{v} \times \vec{w}) = \vec{w} \cdot (\nabla \times \vec{v}) - \vec{v} \cdot (\nabla \times \vec{w})$
$\nabla (\nabla \times \vec{v}) = 0$
$\nabla \times (\nabla f) = 0$
$\nabla^2F := \nabla \cdot (\nabla F)$       - Definition
$\nabla \times (\nabla \times \vec{v}) = \nabla (\nabla \cdot \vec{v}) - \nabla^2\vec{v})$
$\nabla^2 \vec{v} = \langle \nabla^2 v_x, \nabla^2 v_y, \nabla^2 v_z \rangle$     - Definition
$\oint (\nabla F) \cdot \vec{dr} = 0$
$\oint \vec{v} \cdot \vec{dS} = \int (\nabla \cdot \vec{v}) d^3r$
$\oint \vec{v} \cdot \vec{dr} = \int (\nabla \times \vec{v}) \cdot \vec{dS}$

Coordinate Transformations

Cylindrical Coorindates

$\nabla F = \frac{\partial F}{\partial r} \vec{e_r} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial F}{\partial \phi} \vec{e_\phi} + \frac{\partial F}{\partial z} \vec{e_z}$
$\nabla \cdot \vec{v}= \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial (rv_r)}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial v_\phi}{\partial \phi} + \frac{\partial v_z}{\partial z}$
$\nabla^2 F = \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}(r \frac{\partial F}{\partial r}) + \frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial^2F}{\partial \phi^2} + \frac{\partial^2 F}{\partial z^2}$
$\nabla \times F = \left(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial v_z}{\partial \phi} - \frac{\partial v_\phi}{\partial z}\right) \vec{e_r} + \left( \frac{\partial v_r}{\partial z} - \frac{\partial v_z}{\partial r} \right) \vec{e_\phi} + \frac{1}{r}\left(\frac{\partial (rv_\phi)}{\partial r} - \frac{\partial v_r}{\partial \phi}\right) \vec{e_z}$

Note:
$\vec{e_r} = \cos(\phi) \vec{e_x} + \sin(\phi) \vec{e_y}$
$\vec{e_\phi} = -\sin(\phi) \vec{e_x} + \cos(\phi) \vec{e_y}$
Spherical Coordinates

$\nabla F = \frac{\partial F}{\partial r} \vec{e_r} + \frac{1}{r\sin(\theta)}\frac{\partial F}{\partial \phi}\vec{e_\phi} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial F}{\partial \theta} \vec{e_\theta}$
$\nabla F = \frac{\partial F}{\partial r} \vec{e_r} + \frac{1}{r\sin(\theta)}\frac{\partial F}{\partial \phi}\vec{e_\phi} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial F}{\partial \theta} \vec{e_\theta}$
$\nabla \cdot \vec{v} = \frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial (r^2v_r)}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r\sin(\theta)}\frac{\partial v_\phi}{\partial \phi} + \frac{1}{r\sin(\theta)}\frac{\partial (\sin(\theta)v_\theta)}{\partial \theta}$
$\nabla^2 F = \frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial}{\partial r}(r^2\frac{\partial F}{\partial r}) + \frac{1}{r^2\sin(\theta)}\frac{\partial^2 v_\phi}{\partial \phi^2} + \frac{1}{r^2\sin(\theta)}\frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}(\sin(\theta)\frac{\partial F}{\partial \theta})$
$\nabla \times \vec{v} = \frac{1}{r\sin(\theta)}\left( \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta}(\sin(\theta)v_\theta) - \frac{\partial v_\theta}{\partial \phi}\right) \vec{e_r} + \frac{1}{r}\left(\frac{\partial (rv_\theta)}{\partial r} - \frac{\partial v_r}{\partial \theta}\right)\vec{e_\phi} + \frac{1}{r}\left(\frac{1}{\sin(\theta)}\frac{\partial v_r}{\partial \phi} - \frac{\partial (rv_\phi)}{\partial r}\right) \vec{e_\theta}$

Note:
$\vec{e_r} = \sin(\theta)\cos(\phi)\vec{e_x} + \sin(\theta)\sin(\phi)\vec{e_y} + \cos(\theta)\vec{e_z}$ $\vec{e_x} = \sin(\theta)\cos(\phi)\vec{e_r} - \sin(\phi)\vec{e_\phi} + \cos(\theta)\cos(\phi)\vec{e_\theta}$
$\vec{e_\phi} = -\sin(\phi)\vec{e_x} + \cos(\phi)\vec{e_y}$ $\vec{e_y} = \sin(\theta)\sin(\phi)\vec{e_r} + \cos(\phi)\vec{e_\phi} + \cos(\theta)\sin(\phi)\vec{e_\theta}$
$\vec{e_\theta} = \cos(\theta)\cos(\phi)\vec{e_x} + \cos(\theta)\sin(\phi)\vec{e_y} - \sin(\theta)\vec{e_z}$ $\vec{e_z} = \cos(\theta)\vec{e_r} - \sin(\theta)\vec{e_\theta}$
